Comparison to round 1 (delegation scenario)

Round 1 found full-surface beat code-mode on Claude ($1.83 vs $3.13; 3.9 vs 9.2 min) and that small models lose delegation fidelity under code-mode. Round 2 was designed to give code-mode its best shot — a read-heavy aggregation task where mechanized queries should pay off — and code-mode still loses on every axis measured. The direction of the round-1 result reproduces on a completely different task shape, and now on a second harness.

The context blow-up

Context is where the two modes diverge most violently. Codex under code-mode averaged 2.53M tokens against 1.07M on full tools — roughly 2.4×. Claude shows the same direction (515K vs 225K). Code-mode does not “save context by not loading tool schemas”; it spends far more of it iterating on script source, type queries, and result envelopes.

Per-agent gating (Phases 1–2) — validated end-to-end

The claude-scripts-config-r2 cell enabled code-mode only through per-agent swarm_config rows, with the worker container's SCRIPTS_ONLY_MCP env left empty. Its lead ran on script-run ×8 / script-query-types ×5, versus get-tasks / send-task / post-message / memory-search in the full-tools cell. The gating path works; it is the mode itself that does not pay off here.
